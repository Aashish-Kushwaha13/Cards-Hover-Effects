Statue of Liberty

The Statue of Liberty (Liberty Enlightening the World) is a colossal neoclassical sculpture on Liberty Island in New York Harbor in New York City, United States. The Statue was a gift of friendship from the people of France to the United States and is recognized as a universal symbol of freedom and democracy. The Statue of Liberty was dedicated on October 28,18861. It was designated as a National Monument in 1924.

Pyramid

The Great Pyramid of Giza is the largest Egyptian pyramid and served as the tomb of pharaoh Khufu, who ruled during the Fourth Dynasty of the Old Kingdom. The pyramid is the oldest of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World. It is situated at the northeastern end of the line of the three main pyramids at Giza.
